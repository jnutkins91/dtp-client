THE DATA TRADING PLATFORM

Is there a better way to collect data for professional services?

We think so.

Sports betting data is currently supplied by a small number of sports data collection and distribution organisations. These operations are hub-based organisations, with all operations occupying a central data collection site. This is an example of a traditional business model that requires ownership of the assets and data.

The Data Trading Platform takes its inspiration from disruptive companies such as Uber who have revolutionised the Taxi business and Air B&B. which has changed the short-term rental accommodation market. The Data Trading Platform does not own the hardware that collects the data, nor does it own the data. It is simply a lean marketplace for those who want to collect data, process data and use data to create businesses.

The information is gathered by the community, packaged through the Data Trading Platform and bought by users. This provides a highly efficient business model, offering best value and flexible services for customers - coupled with a fair reward for suppliers. Thereby creating a data trading service for the sharing economy (or perhaps more accurately described as the access economy).

These same tools could then be formed into a general-purpose Data Trading Platform.
